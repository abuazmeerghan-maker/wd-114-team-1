Frequently Asked Questions

help Can I upgrade my plan later?

Yes. Members may upgrade from Basic to Standard or from Standard to Premium Plus by submitting a Plan Change Request form. Changes take effect the following quarter.

help When does coverage start?

Coverage begins on the 1st day of the month following your enrollment approval and payment of the first contribution (for Standard and Premium). Basic Coverage starts upon approval of indigent status.

help Can I add my family as dependents?

Yes. Dependents include legal spouse, unmarried children below 21 years old, and parents who are 60 or older with no other source of income. Dependents must be registered through the PMRF.

Application Guide
How to Apply for
Health Insurance Coverage
A step-by-step guide to enrolling in your chosen Health Insurance plan through this portal.
Application Steps
1

Create Your Portal Account

Register using your full legal name, valid email address, and a secure password. You'll need to verify your email before proceeding. Your account is free and permanent — it's used to track all your applications.

2

Select Your Insurance Plan

Navigate to "Insurance Plans" and select the plan that matches your membership category: Basic (indigent), Standard (employed/voluntary), or Premium Plus (OFW/high-income). Each plan has different requirements and benefits.

3

Fill Out the Application Form

Complete all 4 steps of the application: Personal Information → Address → Documents → Schedule & Payment. Make sure all information matches your official government ID to avoid processing delays.

info All fields marked with * are required. Incorrect information may result in rejection.
4

Upload Required Documents

Attach scanned copies or clear photos of your required documents. All documents must be legible. Expired IDs are not accepted. File size limit is 5MB per document (JPG, PNG, or PDF only).

5

Schedule Your Pickup & Pay

Choose your preferred PhilHealth branch, date, and time slot for ID release. For Standard and Premium plans, pay your first month's contribution online via GCash, Maya, or credit/debit card. Basic members do not pay — coverage is government-funded.

warning The person named in the application must be physically present on the scheduled date. Authorized representatives are allowed only with a notarized Special Power of Attorney (SPA).
6

Claim Your PhilHealth ID & MDR

On your scheduled date and time, visit the selected PhilHealth branch and present: valid government ID + your application reference number. You will receive your PhilHealth Member Data Record (MDR) and physical ID card.

Document Requirements by Plan

Basic Plan

  • DSWD Certification of Indigency or 4Ps ID
  • Birth Certificate (PSA copy)
  • Barangay Certificate of Residency
  • Recent 1x1 or 2x2 ID photo
  • PhilSys National ID (if available)

Standard Plan

  • Valid government-issued ID (non-expired)
  • Proof of address (utility bill / barangay cert.)
  • Certificate of Employment or Business Permit
  • BIR Form 2316 or ITR (for income verification)
  • Recent 2x2 ID photo
  • Birth certificates of dependents (if enrolling)

Premium Plus Plan

  • Valid government-issued ID
  • OEC (Overseas Employment Certificate) for OFWs
  • Employment contract or payslip
  • Proof of income (₱100,000+/mo for high-earners)
  • Recent 2x2 ID photo
  • Proof of address abroad (for OFWs)
